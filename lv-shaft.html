What Is the LV Shaft?
Understanding the communication infrastructure backbone of VJ IndiLife Kharadi.

The Building's Nervous System.

The Low Voltage (LV) Shaft is a purpose-built vertical enclosure that runs continuously from the basement to the 35th floor terrace of VJ IndiLife Kharadi. It is the dedicated route for all communication, data, and low-power signal cables in the building.

Think of it as the building's nervous system — carrying all the signals that keep your studio connected, secure, and smart, without interfering with the main power infrastructure.

By keeping low-voltage cables in their own enclosed, protected shaft, VJ IndiLife ensures cleaner signals, easier maintenance, and a building that stays safe and organised for decades.

Cable Systems — In Detail
Every signal type routed through the LV shaft, and what it delivers to your studio.
📶
Broadband / FTTH
Fibre Optic · CAT6 · FTTH Provision
High-speed internet infrastructure runs through the LV shaft from the main distribution frame (MDF) in the basement to a dedicated data point in each unit. Provision for fibre-to-the-home (FTTH) is built in — future-ready from day one. Your router point on the wall connects directly to this cable run.
📺
TV / Cable Antenna
Coaxial · RG-6 · DTH / Cable Ready
A coaxial antenna distribution system runs through the LV shaft, with a tap-off point at each floor. This connects to the TV socket inside your studio. Compatible with cable TV providers and DTH antenna feeds. The wall plate gives you a clean, no-cables-on-the-floor television setup.
☎️
Telephone / Intercom
RJ-11 · Voice Lines · Intercom Wiring
Telephone lines and intercom wiring share the LV shaft, running from the main EPABX panel in the basement to each unit. The telephone jack in your studio wall connects here. The intercom system enables two-way communication between your unit and the building lobby or gate.
🔔
Building Intercom & Buzzer
2-Wire · Video Door Phone Compatible
Dedicated intercom wiring in the LV shaft connects every unit to the building's lobby reception and gate entry. The buzzer point in your unit is fed from this run. Provision for video door phone integration is included — so you can see who is at the entrance from inside your studio.
🔥
Fire Alarm System
Fully Addressable · 24V DC Signal · Fire-Rated Cable
Fire alarm signal cables run through the LV shaft using fire-rated, low-smoke, zero-halogen (LSZH) cables. These connect every smoke detector, heat detector, manual call point, and fire hooter to the main fire alarm control panel (FACP). The system is fully addressable — meaning the exact location of any triggered device is instantly shown at the panel.
📷
CCTV Surveillance
Coaxial / CAT6 · HD Signal · NVR/DVR Connected
Video signal cables from all CCTV cameras — dome cameras in corridors and lobbies, bullet cameras at entrances and perimeter — run through the LV shaft to the central NVR/DVR recording unit in the security room. This enables 24/7 recording and monitoring of all common areas in the building.
ℹ️

Access Card System

Access control wiring for lobby card readers, elevator access control, and restricted floor entry also runs through the LV shaft. This enables the access-controlled lobbies and elevators that are a key security feature of VJ IndiLife Kharadi.

LV Shaft vs. Electrical Shaft
Why complete separation of low-voltage and high-voltage cables is non-negotiable — and what it means for you.
LV Shaft Low Voltage
  • Internet / broadband cables (CAT6, fibre optic)
  • Telephone and intercom wiring (RJ-11)
  • TV antenna / coaxial cable distribution
  • CCTV video signal cables
  • Fire alarm signal cables (LSZH)
  • Access control and intercom lines
  • Maximum voltage: 48V DC signal
  • Runs independently on dedicated route
Electrical Shaft High Voltage
  • Main power supply cables (415V, 3-phase)
  • Distribution Board (DB) feeder cables
  • Lighting circuit risers
  • Power socket circuit cables
  • AC circuit wiring
  • DG backup power cables
  • Voltage: 230V to 415V AC
  • Completely separate route and enclosure
⚡

Why Does Separation Matter to You?

High-voltage power cables generate electromagnetic interference (EMI) that degrades signal quality in data and communication cables. By keeping LV cables in their own dedicated shaft, VJ IndiLife ensures your internet signal is clean and fast, your CCTV footage is clear, your fire alarm system is interference-free, and your intercom works reliably. It also makes fault-finding and maintenance far simpler — a technician can work on the LV shaft without touching anything related to power. This is standard practice in all premium commercial and residential buildings.

Per-Unit Access Point

At each floor, the LV shaft has a protected junction box. From this box, individual cables run in conduit to each unit's internal wall plates — the WiFi/Router point, TV socket, telephone jack, and intercom buzzer point that you see in your studio wall. These conduit runs are concealed within the walls during construction.

Access & Maintenance
How the LV shaft is kept in order — and what that means for service calls in your studio.
🔒
Locked Access Panels
The LV shaft has lockable access panels on each floor — typically in the common corridor. Only authorised building management staff and licensed service technicians can open these. Residents do not have direct access to the shaft.
🛠
Service Provider Visits
When your internet or TV service provider needs to connect or upgrade your line, their technician works from the LV shaft junction box on your floor — not from inside your unit. This keeps service calls minimally intrusive to your space.
🔍
Fault Isolation
Because each cable system is clearly labelled and routed independently within the shaft, faults can be isolated to a specific floor or unit without disrupting other units on the same floor. This dramatically reduces the time taken to fix connectivity issues.
📋
Society Responsibility
The LV shaft and all common-area cabling within it is the responsibility of the Residents' Society after handover. The society's maintenance contract with a licensed electrical contractor typically covers periodic inspection, labelling, and testing of the LV shaft.
⬆️
Future Upgradability
The shaft is sized to accommodate future cable additions — for example, adding a new internet service provider, upgrading to a newer CCTV system, or installing additional access control points. New cables can be pulled through the shaft without major civil work.
🏗
Construction Standard
The shaft enclosure is built with fire-rated walls and doors at each floor penetration, conforming to NBC (National Building Code) requirements. Cable entries through floor slabs are sealed with approved fire-stop material to prevent fire and smoke travel through the shaft.
✅

What This Means for Your Studio

As a unit owner or occupant, the LV shaft is completely transparent to you in daily use. Your WiFi point, TV socket, telephone jack, intercom buzzer, and CCTV coverage all work because of this shaft — reliably, without interference, and with the ability to be serviced or upgraded without disturbing your interior walls.

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions about the LV shaft from VJ IndiLife buyers and owners.
Can I install my own internet cable through the LV shaft? +
No — residents cannot directly access the LV shaft. If you wish to install a new internet service provider or upgrade your connection, your ISP's technician will coordinate with building management to access the shaft junction box on your floor. The internal conduit from the shaft to your unit's wall plate is fixed during construction.
What broadband providers can serve my unit via the LV shaft? +
Any licensed internet service provider (ISP) approved by building management can pull their fibre or cable through the LV shaft to serve your unit. Common providers in Kharadi include Airtel Fibre, Jio Fibre, ACT Fibernet, and others. The shaft infrastructure is provider-agnostic — the building does not lock you into one ISP.
Is the fire alarm in the LV shaft the same system as my unit's sprinkler? +
No — these are two separate systems. The fire alarm signal cables in the LV shaft carry detection signals (from smoke detectors, heat detectors, and manual call points) to the Fire Alarm Control Panel (FACP). The sprinkler system runs via dedicated water pipes in the building's fire-fighting shaft — it is hydraulic, not electrical. The alarm system triggers the hooter; the sprinkler system activates independently based on heat.
Will CCTV cameras monitor the corridor outside my unit? +
Yes — dome cameras are installed in the common corridors on each floor, with their video signal cables running through the LV shaft to the central CCTV recording system. These cover the lift lobby and corridor areas. No CCTV cameras are installed inside individual units — your private space has no surveillance coverage.
How does the access card entry system connect to my floor? +
Each floor lobby has an access control card reader that is wired back to the central access control server in the basement via the LV shaft. When you tap your access card at the lift or lobby reader, the signal travels through the LV shaft to the server, which grants or denies entry. The access card data and logs are managed at the building level by the society.
What is FTTH provision and do I need anything extra to use it? +
FTTH stands for Fibre-to-the-Home. The LV shaft includes a fibre optic conduit run from the basement MDF to each unit's data point on the wall — this is the "provision". To actually use fibre broadband, you need to subscribe to a fibre ISP (like Jio Fibre or Airtel Fibre), who will then pull their fibre cable through this pre-installed conduit route and connect it to an ONT (fibre modem) at your wall point. No civil work is needed in your unit — the route is already there.
